## Executive summary

The Falcon platform comprises **9 active backend services** (.NET 10 across the board, plus one .NET 6 outlier — `T2.PES.API` / Access) totaling roughly **125 catalogued HTTP endpoints**, fronted by **two YARP gateways** (Core Gateway port 7038 for client-tenant users, System Gateway port 7256 for Falcon admins). Both gateways share a 90% YARP pass-through + 10% FastEndpoints aggregation pattern; the Core Gateway additionally carries IP-allowlist enforcement, a per-tenant rate limiter, and a Kafka consumer, while the System Gateway is a thinner shell with no Kafka and no rate limiter. Backend uniformity is strong on the auth (Zitadel JWT Bearer everywhere) and response-wrapper (`ServiceOperationResult<T>` everywhere) intent, but uniformity of *implementation* is weaker: the wrapper has five distinct shapes, two endpoint styles (Controllers + FastEndpoints), two mediator libraries (`MediatR` + `Mediator`), and two mappers (`AutoMapper` + `Mapperly`) split roughly along service-age lines.

The **frontend** workspace (`falcon-web-platform-ui`, Nx 22.7.1, Angular 21.2.9, zoneless, Tailwind v4 only — no SCSS, no PrimeNG) is significantly more mature than the typical pre-rebuild state: a host-shell + two MF remotes (admin-console / management-console), a 47-pair Stencil component library with 49 Angular wrappers (dual-render Shadow + Light DOM), and a working theme-token SSOT. PrimeNG has been physically uninstalled (Wave PR-8). The two source-of-truth gaps that prevent a deeper Brain pass are (a) the **architecture wiki** is missing from `C:\Falcon\falcon-wiki` (Sunday sync stale) and (b) the **PRD folder** is absent at `C:\Falcon\PRD`. Both are deferrable until the next Business or Architecture task, but they should be restored before any large rule-driven refactor lands.
